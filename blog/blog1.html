Understanding Singapore's Career Conversion Programmes (CCP)

Career Conversion Programmes (CCPs) are designed to help Singapore businesses and mid-career employees adapt to a changing economy. Through CCPs, companies receive substantial government funding to train mid-career hires or reskill existing staff into new, in-demand roles. This initiative supports around 30 industry sectors, from technology and finance to healthcare and logistics.

One of the main benefits of a CCP is financial support: the government can subsidize up to 90% of a trainee's salary during the training period. This makes it far more affordable for companies to invest in employee development. The programmes typically involve structured courses and on-the-job training, often provided by accredited training organizations.

There are several CCP pathways. In a "Place-and-Train" model, for example, a company hires an individual for a new role and trains them on the job. In an "Attach-and-Train" model, companies host potential hires for short attachments or courses before making a hiring decision. Another mode allows existing employees to be upskilled for redesigned roles within the company. Each pathway is tailored to different hiring and training scenarios.
