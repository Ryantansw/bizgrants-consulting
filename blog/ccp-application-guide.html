Step by Step Guide: How to Apply for a CCP in Singapore (2025 Edition)

Singapore’s Career Conversion Programme (CCP) helps employers reskill new hires or upskill existing staff, with up to 90% salary support. This guide gives a practical walkthrough based on Workforce Singapore requirements and real advisory experience.

1. Check Your Eligibility

  • Company must be Singapore registered
  • Staff must be Singapore Citizen or PR
  • Role must be new, or show at least 50% change in scope for existing staff

See eligible roles or request an eligibility check.

2. Prepare All Required Documents

  • ACRA Bizfile
  • NRIC (front and back)
  • Employment contract showing new or redesigned role
  • Job description for the redesigned job
  • Salary proof or payslips
  • Declaration forms

3. Develop a Structured OJT Plan

The OJT must include:

  • Role specific learning objectives, tasks and milestones
  • Training duration (typically 3 to 6 months)
  • Assigned supervisor or mentor
  • Clear KPIs and assessment steps

See OJT and reskilling examples

4. Complete the Application Forms

Most CCPs use an online portal. Ensure all fields are complete and supporting documents are attached.

5. Submit and Respond Promptly to Clarifications

  • Submit through the correct programme partner
  • Watch email for clarifications
  • Respond quickly to avoid delays

6. Receive Approval and Implement OJT

Once approved, conduct OJT as planned and track progress for claims. Salary support reimbursements are typically processed quarterly.

Key Questions for Employers

  • Q: How do I speed up approval?
    A: Prepare documents early, ensure OJT is role specific, and respond fast to clarifications.
  • Q: Can CCP be combined with other grants?
    A: Yes. CCP is often paired with Job Redesign Grant and SFEC. See comparison.
  • Q: What causes rejections?
    A: Generic job scopes, unclear OJT, missing documents. See common pitfalls.
