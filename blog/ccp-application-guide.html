Step-by-Step Guide: How to Apply for a CCP in Singapore

Introduction

Applying for a Career Conversion Programme (CCP) can seem daunting, but with a clear roadmap, Singapore companies can successfully tap into generous salary support to future-proof their workforce. Here’s a practical, step-by-step guide based on the latest requirements.

1. Assess Your Company’s Eligibility

  • Is your business registered and operating in Singapore?
  • Do you have eligible Singaporean or PR staff for reskilling, or are you planning to hire?
  • Will the new role involve at least 50% change in job scope (for existing staff)?

2. Choose the Right CCP Pathway

CCPs exist for many sectors (e.g., Digital, Sustainability, Finance, HR). Identify the most relevant programme and understand its specific criteria. You can browse the full list here.

3. Prepare the Required Documentation

  • Company ACRA/Bizfile and HR contact details
  • Job description for the redesigned/new role
  • Employee’s latest CV, NRIC, and employment contract
  • Training plan (OJT and external, where required)

4. Submit the Application

Applications are typically submitted via the appointed programme manager. Each manager has its own process—ensure all forms are complete and all supporting documents are clear.

5. Respond to Queries & Provide Clarifications

Be prepared for follow-up questions from assessors. Common issues include unclear job redesign, insufficient documentation, or mismatch in employee background.

6. Receive Approval and Commence Programme

  • Upon approval, salary support will be disbursed in tranches after evidence of completion is submitted.
  • OJT must be tracked and documented.
  • Retain all supporting evidence for audit purposes.
