Common Pitfalls in CCP Applications—and How to Avoid Rejection

1. Vague or Generic Job Descriptions

Applications with copy-pasted, generic JDs are frequently rejected. Assessors want to see clear evidence of job redesign and specific, measurable new responsibilities.

2. Insufficient or Incomplete Documentation

  • Missing or expired ACRA/Bizfile extracts
  • Unsigned employment contracts
  • Incomplete OJT or training plans
  • Unclear reporting lines or mismatched supporting documents

3. Not Meeting the 50% Job Content Change

For existing staff, if you cannot show at least 50% change in tasks and responsibilities, your application will be rejected. Use before-and-after task mapping to demonstrate this shift.

4. Unqualified Candidates

Staff must be Singaporeans/PRs, not company shareholders or family of directors, and not returning ex-employees within 6 months. Ensure all eligibility checks are done before submission.

5. Ignoring Assessor Queries

Respond promptly and clearly to clarifications from programme managers or assessors. Delays or vague replies risk rejection or missed funding deadlines.
