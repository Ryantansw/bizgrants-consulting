Common Pitfalls in CCP Applications and How to Avoid Rejection

Preparing a Career Conversion Programme application can be challenging. Many employers unintentionally make mistakes that slow down approval or lead to rejection. Here are the issues we see most often, and how to avoid them.

Top 7 Reasons CCP Applications Are Rejected

  1. Incomplete documents: Missing NRIC, ACRA, contracts or declaration forms.
  2. Generic job descriptions: Not tailored to the redesigned role.
  3. Poorly structured OJT plan: No clear learning outcomes, milestones or measurable tasks.
  4. Insufficient role change: Below 50% change for existing staff.
  5. Ineligible employee: Shareholders, immediate family of directors, or previously funded for the same role.
  6. Slow clarifications: Delayed responses to queries.
  7. Lack of implementation: OJT not actually carried out, risking clawback.

How to Avoid These Pitfalls

  • Prepare documents early
  • Use company specific and role based language
  • Reference SkillsFuture or sectoral frameworks
  • Assign an experienced supervisor or mentor
  • Respond promptly to clarifications
  • Document all OJT delivery for claims

FAQ for Employers

  • Q: What should a job description include?
    A: Real tasks, responsibilities, deliverables and KPIs for the redesigned role.
  • Q: Is a generic OJT plan acceptable?
    A: No. Each OJT must be tailored to the specific role and skills required.
