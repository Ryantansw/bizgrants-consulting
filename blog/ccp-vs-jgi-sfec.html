Maximising Workforce Singapore Funding: CCP vs Other Salary Support Schemes

Introduction

Singapore employers can tap on a variety of salary support schemes for hiring, reskilling, and workforce transformation. The three main ones—CCP, JGI, and SFEC—each serve different purposes. Here’s how they compare, and how you can make the most of them.

Career Conversion Programme (CCP)

  • Salary support for hiring new mid-career talent or reskilling existing staff into redesigned roles
  • Support up to 90% of monthly salary (for SMEs) over 3 to 6 months
  • Focuses on genuine job conversion, structured OJT, and workforce renewal
  • Requires clear job redesign and documented training plans

Jobs Growth Incentive (JGI)

  • Automatic wage support for employers who increase local headcount
  • Focuses on new hires and increases in net employment
  • Less paperwork, but scheme is time-limited and has phased reductions
  • Can be stacked with CCP if eligibility is met (but not for the same wage period)

SkillsFuture Enterprise Credit (SFEC)

  • One-off credit for companies to offset costs of workforce transformation (training, technology, job redesign)
  • Automatically applied if you meet eligibility (usually after certain contributions and spending)
  • Can be used to co-fund course fees, equipment, and consultancy—helpful for implementing CCPs and upskilling plans

Which to Use First?

  • For job redesign and staff reskilling: Start with CCP
  • For simply growing local headcount: Tap on JGI if available
  • For offsetting additional costs (training, consultancy): Use SFEC as a bonus “top up”

Our Recommendation

Most growth-oriented SMEs in Singapore should structure hiring and job redesign plans to leverage CCP first, with JGI as a booster if eligible, and SFEC as a way to stretch every dollar.
