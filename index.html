Funding overview

What are Career Conversion Programmes and job redesign grants?

Career Conversion Programmes (CCPs) and job redesign grants are Workforce Singapore and SkillsFuture initiatives that help employers reskill or upskill staff, hire mid-career professionals and redesign existing jobs for digital, sustainability and business transformation.

How employers typically benefit

  • Receive up to 70% to 90% salary support for eligible staff on structured on-the-job training.
  • Reduce hiring and training costs when building new capabilities in digital, sustainability, tech, HR and more.
  • Tap into a broader mid-career talent pool while maintaining a sustainable payroll.
  • Align job redesign with real business needs instead of purely HR-driven initiatives.

Who is typically eligible?

  • Singapore-registered companies hiring or reskilling Singapore Citizens or PRs.
  • Roles that are newly created or redesigned with at least 50 percent change in job content.
  • Mid-career individuals aged 21 and above moving into new or significantly expanded roles.
  • Staff who are not shareholders or immediate family members of directors.

Next step

If you are hiring or redesigning roles in the next 3 to 6 months, it is often best to review CCP options early. We can run a quick eligibility check across your planned hires and existing staff and highlight viable pathways.

Common questions from employers

Frequently asked questions about CCP and job redesign grants

These are typical questions we receive from HR teams, business owners and hiring managers when they first explore CCP and related grants.

Who is eligible for the Career Conversion Programme (CCP)?

Most Singapore-registered companies hiring or reskilling Singapore Citizens or PRs into new or redesigned roles may be eligible for CCP funding. The specifics differ across programme partners and job families, so we usually start with a quick eligibility discussion based on your planned hires and existing staff.

How much salary support can my company receive?

Depending on the CCP and your SME status, employers may receive up to 70 percent to 90 percent salary support for the programme duration. We help you frame the job scope, structured OJT and documentation so the application clearly aligns with programme criteria.

What types of roles are usually supported?

In practice we see a wide range of roles, including digital marketing, sustainability, HR and people roles, sales and business development, operations, tech and product, and specialised engineering roles. The key is that there is a clear conversion in skills, industry or job scope.

How do we get started with an application?

Most employers begin with a short call to map their planned hires and internal reskilling needs against the different CCP pathways. From there, we prepare a simple action plan, documentation checklist and timeline for each potential application.
