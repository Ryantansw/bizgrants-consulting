Career Conversion Programmes (CCPs)

Career Conversion Programmes (CCPs) are government-supported initiatives by Workforce Singapore to help companies reskill mid-career new hires or existing employees into new growth job roles. CCPs provide substantial funding support – for example, up to 90% salary subsidy during training – across roughly 30 industry sectors. These programmes follow structured pathways, often involving industry-recognized training and on-the-job learning, to equip workers with in-demand skills.

CCP Modes

There are three main modes of CCPs:

  • Place-and-Train: Companies hire mid-career employees and provide on-the-job training or courses in new job roles.
  • Attach-and-Train: Companies host potential hires for attachments or training before formal placement.
  • Job Redesign Reskilling: Existing employees are sent for reskilling to take on redesigned or new roles aligned with industry needs.
